PRODUCT DESCRIPTION


 

The CDK3-SP Corner Docks is a collection of 2 corner style lots, an inner & an outer corner... that allow you to extend your Seaport compex around corners. Both lots are 5x5 in size... and both are fully transit enabled for rail.

The Inner Corner Dock is a ploppable IND-M lot with about 50 jobs. It is transit enabled in all 4 directions to allow visual connections to rail directly to the lot or across roads, avenues, etc..  Like the Container Dock lot, the the transit enabling was made functional for extra flexibility. As 2 transit enabled lots can not be plopped next to each other, most of the other CDK3-SP lots are not transit enabled. because of this limitation, rail within the CDK3-SP is primarily for appearances only.


The  Outer Corner Dock is similar to the Innner Corner lot in most regards except that it can not have direct road access and therefore does not offer any jobs.

Both lots use a variety of timed and random props for visual variety. Stacks of containers change size & shape throughout the day & container rail cars & trucks come and go. Both lots are designed to act as container holding or marshalling areas... with containers up on the docks that could be staged for ships that have their own cranes.

INSTALLATION
If installing from the ZIP archive, download the file to your C:\My Documents\SimCity 4\Plugins folder. Then, unzip the file making sure that the "Use Folder Names" option is selected. The product will be installed in an appropriate folder under the PEGPROD sub-folder which will be created if it does not already exist on your system. Once installed, the product will be ready to use in your game. Lots will appear in the menu listed in the above Product Details section.
** After installation, you can either delete the downloaded ZIP file or archive it outside of your Plugins folder satructure.
DEVELOPER NOTES

The lot is a CDK3 lot and therefore must be plopped over water. It has been designed to have the inner most 3 rows of tiles plopped fully on dry land.

The Inner Corner lot may be difficult to plop and/or difficult to connect to rail if the area is not perfectly level. By default, the lot will attempt to plop to sea-level... but its rail conections will attempt to level the lot to the height of any adjacent rail lines. It is advisable to first plop the lot with no rail nearby... then attempt to connect the rail.

The rail connections are purely optional and do not affect the functionality of the lot in anyway. At night, the rolling chain link gates close across the rail entrances... visually closing them off.
