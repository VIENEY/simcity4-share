PRODUCT DESCRIPTION

w

As Mayor, you have done such a fine job that the Navy is just tickled pink (well... grey, actually) and is looking at your city as the ideal location to build a base for their reserve squadron of destroyers. These old "Greyhounds of the Sea" may be a tad long in the tooth but they are still a vital part of our maritime defense strategy. Like their namesakes, they may be a bit old but they are well maintained, very hardy and just as functional & productive as the day they slid down the ways.

This Navy Reserve Center is the 3rd in the Navy Base chain of conditional rewards with each subsequent base requiring just a bit more of your mayoral skills. It has features and properties similar to the first 2 Naval bases and fits on a 8x4 tile lot. It is transit enabled &  a moderate polluter... but provides jobs to civilian workers as well as income to the city each month. It's designed to be compatible with the CDK but can be used separately.

Using the new CDKN building methodology that is utilized by all new naval facilities, this third Navy Pier brings the base to life with new buildings and dockside props and personnel that shuffle & change throughout the day. The two piers are actual a single base model utilizing separate "veneer" models to provide different cargo & personnel details on each pier.

Each subsequent base will require a little more mayoral effort and city development before becoming available. This base requires a city population of over 30,000, a mayor rating of 45+, Seaport usage over 75, a city that is at least 17% water (still notably less than the requirement for the Marina)... or completion of the good Fishing Boat Mission. The lot's dynamic icon info lists these requirements as well as the current levels of each requirement so you can see how close you are getting.. Once earned, the base is free to plop and will generate §400 a month for your city's coffers.

In keeping with the time honored tradition of naming vessels after notable individuals within the Community, we pay homage to BarbyW, Mrbisonm, Vlakhaas, GrandpaAl & Beowulf60 for their steady contributions and involvement in the community... and because each of them are easily as old... um, er...  as "seasoned" as these vessels.

The "tin cans" depicted on this lot are WWII era U.S. Fletcher-class destroyers. In 1941, the US Navy began building a fleet of large destroyers, its first design to rival the Japanese special type destroyers that had entered service beginning more than a decade before. Introduced in 1942, the Fletcher-class destroyers were mainstays of the post-1930 destroyer fleet from 1943.

The United States built 175 of the Fletcher-class destroyers which were, in retrospect  the most successful of all American destroyers of that era. INSTALLATION

From the setup .exe:
Nothing. If you are reading this, you're done.

From the zip file:
Unzip the file to your C:\My Documents\SimCity 4\Plugins folder making sure the zip "Use Folder Names" option is selected. The product will be installed in the appropriate folder under the PEGPROD sub-folder which will be created if it does not already exist on your system. If any earlier version(s) of this file exist, they should be deleted.

Once installed, the lots will be available in the menus indicated above.

DEVELOPER NOTES

This lot will plop like any CDK lot.  The lot is only 4 tiles deep by the piers and ships project well out into the water... so allow room for the 'over-hang'. The ships and piers will not interfere with the path of water automata such as ferries and cargo ships... which will travel through them. Use care in selecting where you plop the base to avoid having other vessels run through the piers and ships.

Using the CDK Warehouse lot works well as a spacer between CDKN naval facilities. The lot was designed for use with the PEG CDK Industrial style and the new CDK Transition lots that blend the shallower (2 tile deep) lots with the larger (4 tile deep) CDK and CDKN (naval) lots. The lot can be plopped with either one or two rows extending into the water for greater flexibility.

* Use of the PEG CDK Leveler Tool will make the lot and the surrounding area easier to plop and use. 

NOTE -  These vessels are static and will not "deploy" or operate as game automata.
