RCI Query 'Upgrade' Mod
 Written and Conceptualized by Equinox.
STEX Upload Version 1.2 (11-November-2004)
 

This document covers two main things: one, how to both install this mod and use it; two, how to read the extra information that has been newly-added to the queries.

This is a mod I wrote after becoming frustrated with the demand cap situation in one of my cities. I realized that I had no idea what the cap was until my desirability started to fall and I began to see dilapidated and abandoned buildings. So, I took the game's default commercial, industrial, and residential queries and began adding information to the bottom of them, eventually coming up with what you see here.

 


Part I: Installation and Setup
 

Extract the 3 DAT files within the zip file to the following folder (if you installed SimCity 4 to the default location):

C:\Program Files\Maxis\SimCity 4\Plugins

Open that folder and make sure there are three new files, "Commercial Building UI (CA56783A).dat", "Industrial Building UI (2A567DC1).dat", and "Residential Building UI (4A5672BF).dat". These are the files for the correlating query replacements. The code in parenthesis is the Instance ID of the UI file - not important unless you want to fiddle with the UI files in the game.

The other files in the zip archive are not nessecary. They are simply screenshots and this readme. Leaving them in your plugins folder will cause no harm, though; you might want to refer back to this document for information regarding caps, etc.

 


Part II: Query Statistics
 

Demand Caps and Active Demand

Demand caps are items that essentially oppose the growth of your city and if not properly addressed can dry up all your city's demand. Caps are fixed ceilings beyond which a population cannot grow, regardless of the demand for that developer type. With any increase in the population, you get closer to the cap, and the amount of demand allowed decreases until you reach the cap, at which point the demand becomes zero regardless of what is occurring in your city. Most developer types are restricted by demand caps. The developer types NOT affected by this are CS§, CS§§, and CS§§§. Note that demand caps are city-specific.

The percentages displayed in the query simply tell you what percent of your current cap is filled. Higher percentages mean lower demand. Also, related is the "Active Demand" variable displayed in the queries. This is the demand, in # of Sims (I think), that is desired in conjunction with your cap status.

Caps can be relieved by plopping certain civic structures or some of the lots on the Simtropolis Exchange. The easier way to relieve these caps, though, is to create neighbor connections, and lots of them. The amount of relief a connection provides decreases with each one you make, but are net-work-specific. Commercial and industrial desire connections to neighboring cities, and residential desires amenities (parks, etc.). For full listing of cap relief structures and how much each plop provides, check out a strategy guide to the game.

 

"All the Other Statistics"

The other statistics are not as important or useful, but they do provide some insight as to how you develop your city. I find it interesting to look at the relationship of the number of high, medium, and low density cells I have zoned ... it is always skewed in the HD direction. ;)

The number of jobs versus the actual occupance ("Occupancy" and "Capacity", respectively) is merely an indicator of how much you fill your buildings. If demand is very high in each city and you are not providing many new tracts, this number will be closer to the capacity. The reverse is true as well.

The generic demand indicator is the # of Sims (again, I *think*) in demand with all effects of caps removed.

The only other new statistic included in the query is your actual numeric citywide mayor rating in the residential query, right next to the "Mayor Rating" label.
