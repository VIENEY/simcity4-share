PRODUCT DESCRIPTION

The original PEG naval facilities are back!!  This initial offering features an all-new Battleship naval facility that has been re-worked from the ocean floor up... and offers a zillion new props and features. This new base establishes a completely new lot building methodology (CDKN) that will be incorporated into all subsequent naval facilities as well as all new CDK lots.

For starters, this new base (Navy Pier One) is a conditional reward... like the game's army and air force bases.  Each subsequent base will require a little more mayoral effort and city development before becoming available. This base requires a city population of over 30,000, a mayor rating of 45+, Seaport usage over 50, a city that is at least 10% water (notably less than the requirement for the Marina)... or completion of the good Fishing Boat Mission. The lot's dynamic icon info lists these requirements as well as the current levels of each requirement so you can see how close you are getting.. Once earned, the base is free to plop and will generate §400 a month for your city's coffers.

The ship models remain the same except that they will now deploy to sea. Instead of being tied up to the pier all the time, they will now leave and return at various times throughout the year... simulating actual deployments and cruises.

The bases also now have shuffling, time-activated props that add more variety and life to the lot. Various cargo and personnel will shuffle around throughout the day... eventually thinning out and disappearing in the evening hours.

One of the biggest changes is the new modular seawall/foundation construction that replaces the original 1 piece foundations... and reduces the total file size by almost half (from 2+ megs to a bit over 1 meg). The seawall components are smaller and can be mixed and matched to form unlimited variations of seawalls/piers... of virtually any size. These are contained in a re-useable prop pack (included with this download) that can be re-used by all subsequent bases... further reducing download times substantially and improving game performance.

We will continuing the time honored tradition of naming vessels after notable individuals... and commission this ship as the S.C.S. (SimCity Ship) Bumpy Johnson in honor of his unrelenting efforts to improve our on-line community. As is our practice, there are no in-game references to ship names or nationality so players can easily incorporate them into city journals and/or storylines in any manner they choose.

History Snippet: The first modern battleship or "dreadnought" was the H.M.S. Dreadnought built in 1907.  The name "Dreadnought" was taken from the family motto of Admiral 'Jackie' Fisher who was the First Sea Lord largely responsible for her building. That motto was 'Fear God But Dread Nought'... which he latter coined in the now famous phrase, 'Fear God... But Dreadnought'

The construction of the H.M.S. Dreadnought sparked an unparalleled warship development race, primarily between Britain and Germany... which resulted in her being all but obsolete at the outbreak of World War One in 1914. The H.M.S. Dreadnought missed the Battle of Jutland in 1917 being laid up for an overhaul. However, she is the only battleship ever to have sunk a submarine... having rammed and sank the German U-29 in 1915.

She was broken up for scrap in 1923.  


INSTALLATION

From the setup .exe:
Nothing. If you are reading this, you're done.

From the zip file:
Unzip the file to your C:\My Documents\SimCity 4\Plugins folder making sure the zip "Use Folder Names" option is selected. The product will be installed in the appropriate folder under the PEGPROD sub-folder which will be created if it does not already exist on your system. If any earlier version(s) of this file exist, they should be deleted.

Once installed, the lots will be available in the menus indicated above.

DEVELOPER NOTES

This lot will plop like any CDK lot.  The lot is 10 tiles long and the ship is 12 tiles long... so allow room for the 'over-hang'. The lot was designed for use with the PEG CDK Industrial style and the new CDK Transition lots that blend the shallower (2 tile deep) lots with the larger (4 tile deep) CDK and CDKN (naval) lots. The lot can be plopped with either one or two rows extending into the water for greater flexibility.

* Use of the PEG CDK Leveler Tool will make the lot and the surrounding area easier to plop and use. 

 The ship will likely not appear when the lot is first plopped. This is normal as the ship is at sea. Once the base is constructed (plopped), the ship will steam to it and arrive at the end of it's current deployment or cruise... generally within a few months.
