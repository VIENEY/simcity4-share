PRODUCT DESCRIPTION

w

The Navy likes it so much here, that they have decided to expand their port to include a new AEGIS Command Center. This new pier will be home to the most awesome and sophisticated vessels ever to sail in Harm's Way.

Imagine a destroyer the size of a WWII cruiser... and packing more firepower than a battleship. Match that up with the AEGIS weapons and defense system that is so TOP SECRET, that if we said anything more, we'd have to kill you. And considering how many downloads this lot will get, that would get rather messy!

This is an all new Naval Base with features and properties similar to the Navy Pier One lot. Its designed to be compatible with the CDK but can be used separately.  As with the Navy Pier One lot, it is a conditional reward with slightly higher requirements. Its transit enabled, a moderate polluter... but provides jobs to civilian workers as well as income to the city each month.

Using the new CDKN building methodology that is utilized by all new naval facilities, this second Navy Pier brings the base to life with new buildings and dockside props and personnel that shuffle & change throughout the day. The ship also deploys throughout the year so don't be surprised when you notice its gone. Don't worry... its a stout ship. It will be back...

Each subsequent base will require a little more mayoral effort and city development before becoming available. This base requires a city population of over 20,000, a mayor rating of 45+, Seaport usage over 75, a city that is at least 15% water (still notably less than the requirement for the Marina)... or completion of the good Fishing Boat Mission. The lot's dynamic icon info lists these requirements as well as the current levels of each requirement so you can see how close you are getting.. Once earned, the base is free to plop and will generate §400 a month for your city's coffers.

We are continuing the time honored tradition of naming vessels after notable individuals... and commission this ship as the S.C.S.  Andreas in honor of his numerous contributions to this gaming community and his continuous efforts in supporting numerous lot developers and administering multiple fansites. As is our practice, there are no in-game references to ship names or nationality so players can easily incorporate them into city journals and/or storylines in any manner they choose.

History Snippet:

Beginning in WWII at the Battle of Leyte Gulf and later in Okinawa, the longest and most decimating battle in Navy history, the success of the Kamikaze in attacking surface ships was obvious to all. Later in the mid-east, the early anti-ship cruise missiles, although crude by current standards, also proved to be very effective.

 

Beginning in the 1950’s and 1960’s, there were many who argued that the days of surface Navies were over – that surface ships could no longer survive in combat, even against an unsophisticated enemy.  Thus the motivation for developing a shipboard defense system that could counter both aircraft and missiles. Aegis represented a major transformation, in which the ship, the combat systems and the training systems were designed as a single unit.

 

The AEGIS system was designed as a total weapons system, from detection to kill. The heart of the system is an advanced, automatic detect and track, multi-function phased-array radar. This high-powered radar is able to perform search, track and missile guidance functions simultaneously with a track capacity of over 100 targets.  The core of the Aegis combat system is the computer-based command and decision element. This interface makes the Aegis combat system capable of simultaneous operation against a multi-mission threat: anti-air, anti-surface and anti-submarine warfare.  


INSTALLATION

From the setup .exe:
Nothing. If you are reading this, you're done.

From the zip file:
Unzip the file to your C:\My Documents\SimCity 4\Plugins folder making sure the zip "Use Folder Names" option is selected. The product will be installed in the appropriate folder under the PEGPROD sub-folder which will be created if it does not already exist on your system. If any earlier version(s) of this file exist, they should be deleted.

Once installed, the lots will be available in the menus indicated above.

DEVELOPER NOTES

This lot will plop like any CDK lot.  The lot is 8 tiles long and the ship is slightly longer... so allow room for the 'over-hang'. Using the CDK Warehouse lot works well as a spacer between CDKN naval facilities. The lot was designed for use with the PEG CDK Industrial style and the new CDK Transition lots that blend the shallower (2 tile deep) lots with the larger (4 tile deep) CDK and CDKN (naval) lots. The lot can be plopped with either one or two rows extending into the water for greater flexibility.

* Use of the PEG CDK Leveler Tool will make the lot and the surrounding area easier to plop and use. 

NOTE -  The ship will likely not appear when the lot is first plopped. This is normal as the ship is at sea. Once the base is constructed (plopped), the ship will steam to it and arrive at the end of it's current deployment or cruise... generally within a few months. (game time)
